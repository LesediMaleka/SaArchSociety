6 Degree Architects is a Johannesburg-based inter-disciplinary design studio, founded by Yusuf Patel and Adam Osman in 2020. Yusuf and Adam are two recent architecture graduates from the Graduate School of Architecture (GSA) at the University of Johannesburg.

 They are both invested in designing and enhancing emerging South African conditions at all levels of human social, cultural, and artistic endeavors, with an aim of developing a uniquely Johannesburg and African design language. Our firm is dedicated to architecture, urbanism, interior design and research.

Our clients and projects are diverse in nature and range from small home renovations to large inner-city developments as well as social outreach cases. We are currently partnered with Jewel City and are working on a project in Maboneng assisting artists in transforming the precinct with murals on the facades of multiple buildings.We are also involved in pro bono work and are currently designing a centre for abused women and children in the South of Johannesburg.

It is evident from the scale of projects that 6Degrees is involved with that they are a motivated team willing produce innovative solutions to new take on challenges.

Adam Osman is a Candidate Architect born and raised in Cape Town. He later moved to Johannesburg where he graduated from the University of Johannesburg, receiving a Masters with Distinction from Unit 14: Rogue Economies. His interests lie in how Johannesburg is shaped in dramatic ways by its economic practices, tactics and transactions.

Following his Master’s Degree, he held a position at Urban Works Architecture, while also tutoring within Unit 14. At present, he works for the award-winning practice Counterspace Studio and is completing his second Master’s Degree in Design and Theory, as well as tutoring Unit 18: Hyperreal Prototypes.

Yusuf Patel is a Candidate Architect born and raised in Johannesburg. He graduated from the University of Johannesburg, receiving his Master's from Unit 14: Rogue Economies. He specialises in high end residential and commercial architecture but has a keen interest in social housing.

Yusuf has experience working for the award-winning firm 26'10’ South Architects and Bjala Urban Developers , an urban social enterprise , developing solutions to urbanisation challenges. Currently, he is employed by AMPM Projects and is in pursuit of his second Master’s Degree in Design and Theory.
